About

The orange heart project mixes art and Dweb technology through a heart-centered perspective to explore addressing and resilience. The gallery component presents stencil-based spray art on birchwood.

Stencils create a form of visual consistency that builds upon itself through repeated reuse.

In terms of re-purposing, the same stencil is getting used in two different ways. The gallery show is a series of images created by the stencil, whereas the online component includes photographs of the world through the stencil.

Given that one of the features of decentralized technologies, such as IPFS, is replication, I am intentionally using the same stencil even though it creates a sequence of repetitive images. Over the time, art degrade on any surface whether it is concrete or paper. As you reuse the stencil, it does loose its sharpness as it picks up paint.

Yas Etessam (she/her)

Yas Etessam is a markup technologist. She’s been coding since the 80’s and has expertise in enterprise content.

Yas has had the opportunity to work alongside a variety of technology industry giants in semiconductor, enterprise software, cloud computing, networking, telecommunications, and artificial intelligence. Most recently, Yas served as a Product Owner on an enterprise Content Pipeline team and chaired the applicable product information governance board.

Darwin Information Typing Architecture (DITA) is the foundational standard for user-centric information architecture, as it is flexible and adaptable to change. Yas notably contributed to DITA adoption as a significant contributor on the team that developed the first commercially available DITA editor, the XMetaL DITA Edition, and to key discussions and policy decisions on the OASIS Technical Committee. She has frequently shared her knowledge at industry events and provided technical input to DITA Best Practices: A Roadmap for Writing, Editing, and Architecting in DITA by Laura Bellamy, Michelle Carey, et al. published by IBM Press.

Currently, Yas is experimenting with integrating decentralized technologies and AI into a DITA content pipeline. Outside of markup, Yas loves travel, art, music, and dance and frequently finds herself supporting social movements for positive change.
