About

The orange heart project mixes art and Dweb technology through a heart-centered perspective to explore addressing and resilience. The gallery component presents stencil-based spray art on birchwood.

Why stencils? They're a visual language. They are reusable. It's a fast form of art that doesn't require studio space.

This project repurposes a heart-shaped stencil created as a response to gun violence in San Francisco's Mission District. I have been carrying around this paper plate for a long time. It's been used to make holiday cards, make art pieces and gifts, cover up "HATE", lament gun violence on Capp st, defy bullies, all of this from a cookie cutter stencil. Online the photos in the gallery, it's used to create a heart-shaped perspective. Over time, its edges have softened and developed cracks and that's okay.
